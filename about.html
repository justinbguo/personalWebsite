At a Professional Glance
When I was younger, the entrepreneurship bug bit me, and that rash never went away. I obsessively took notes watching Shark Tank, listened to business podcasts, and tried to find little side hustles here and there. As I grew up, I developed an intuition for using technology and began to recognize its positive value to users.

Now that I'm older, I've realized a high interest in another area within the tech space: product management. PM to me is like a different, standardized variation of entrepreneurship that offers a similar thrill of building products and working with awesome people with lower levels of stress and risk.

I love the idea of building features that anybody could understand and benefit from. I find the challenge of simplifying a deeply rooted painpoint into a layman's solution to be highly exciting. With the right implementation, users can derive a high amount of positive impact from even the smallest of product improvements.


My strengths lie in creativity, critical thinking, autonomy, personability, and curiosity.
